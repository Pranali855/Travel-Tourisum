Tirupati Balaji Temple

Tirupati Balaji Temple, located in Tirumala, Andhra Pradesh, is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. It is one of the richest and most visited temples in the world, often called Kaliyuga Vaikuntha. Built and expanded by Pallava, Chola, Pandya, and Vijayanagara kings, the temple is famous for its sacred Tirupati Laddu prasadam. Devotees also offer their hair as a symbol of devotion. The grand Brahmotsavam festival attracts millions of pilgrims every year.
