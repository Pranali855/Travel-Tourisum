Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is located in Uttarakhand at 3,583 meters near the Mandakini River. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and part of the Chota Char Dham Yatra, open only from April to November due to heavy snow. According to legend, the Pandavas built it after the Mahabharata war, and Adi Shankaracharya rebuilt it in the 8th century. Shiva is worshipped here in the form of a hump-shaped lingam. The stone temple survived the 2013 floods and is reached by a 16 km trek from Gaurikund or by helicopter. Pilgrims believe visiting grants moksha.
